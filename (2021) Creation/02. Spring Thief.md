#### MV Notes
In the MV at around the [4:24 mark](https://youtu.be/Sw1Flgub9s8&t=264s), it shows a book with haikus on it. [theyleaveshadows](https://www.reddit.com/user/theyleaveshadows/) tried translating them [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1XnNEAIT0vi4tvIVim2YROmiCGHx-v2lH-IdjqK015L8/).
```
おぼろなり彫りし葡萄は金にして

oboro nari horishi budou wa kin ni shite

Engrave the clouded[hazy, dim] things; make grapes into gold


春の闇罩めあしたまで扉をとざす

haru no yami kome ashita made tobira wo tozasu

Enshroud [Put in, include] the darkness of Spring, and lock the door until tomorrow [*]

*[とざす has another definition, which is "to consume (with negative feelings), to fill (e.g. with sadness), to bury (e.g. in grief)"  Maybe an alternate reading might be like, Include the darkness of spring, and [you will] fill the door towards tomorrow with [sadness, grief, etc etc].]


春の灯は艦をつくりしドックにはなき

haru no hi wa kan wo tsukurishi DOCKS ni wa naki

The lights of Spring illuminates [creates] warships[vessels], even without docks

[The light of spring illuminates warships. As for the docks, they lament.]


めつむりて闇(暗)きが中に白牡丹

me tsumurite kuraki ga chuu ni shiro botan

Shut your eyes [feign ignorance], and in the middle of that darkness will be a white peony


ふくよかの牡丹は顔に雨の粒

fukuyoka no botan wa kao ni ame no tsubu

Plump peonys have a drop of rain on their faces


白牡丹寵愛しけりゆるされよ

shirobotan chouai shikeri yurusare yo

I have dampened my love, [the love of/my love of] the white peonies - please forgive me[them]


Shitty alternatives
[Dampen and forgive the white peonies]
[White peonies, I have dampened my love. Please forgive me]


石濡れて一輪の牡丹うつしけり

ishinurete ichirin no botan utsushikeri

The wet stones reflect a single peony


ただ一輪老残とこそ白牡丹

Tada ichiwarou zan to koso shiro botan

Surely a single old man is like a white peony

(一輪 (ichirin) is also an archaism for full moon, apparently :think:)


春信がゑがく山吹わが庭に

Harunobu ga / we gaku yamabuki / waga niwa ni

Harunobu (Suzuki) paints a picture of yamabuki*, within my garden

*Japanese kerria, Japanese marigold


濃山吹蝶蜂よぎるものは消ゆ

koyamabuki chou hachi yogiru mono wa ke yu

Koyamabuki* - that which butterflies and bees pass by will disappear

*variety of yamabuki, according to weblio
```
